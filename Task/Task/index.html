What is trade mark statement of use?

A Statement of use is a declaration that is filled by a trademark applicant
starting that the applicant is using their trademark on goods and/or
services that are available for sale in interstate commerce.

If trademark applicants aren't using their mark in commerce, the applicant
can file an intent to use Application anf generally if all goes well, receive
a Notice of Allowance. Within six months of being granted the Notice of
Allowance, the applicant generally has to prove that it is now using the
mark or request up to five six-month extensions. The UPSTO requires a
specimen to prove use in commerce when a Statement of Use is filed.

Trademark Statement of use FAQs

A trademark Statement of Use (SOU) is a legal document used to demonstrate that a trademark is being used in commerce and is required for trademark registration application based on intent-to-use, solifying your brand's launch into the marketplace and its protection.

A trademark is a word, symbol, design, or combination of these that identifies a company's products or service. It's also known as a brand name.

For an accepted "Statement Of Use" in a trademark application, you need to verify the trademark's use in commerce, provide a statement of the date of first use and goods/services, and submit adequate specimens, according to trademark Factory.

For an "intent-to-use" (ITU) trademark application to convert to a valid registration, the deadline to file a Statement of Use(SOU) is within six months of the Notice of Allowance(NOA) issuance date. You can extend this timeframe with appropriate requests, though it requires paying an extension fee.

The fee to file a Statement of Use for a trademark in India is INR 2,700 per class. You'll need to pay this fee for each class of goods or services covered by your trademark application.

For your Trademark Statement of Use (SOU), you must submit a sworn statement about the first use date of the trademark, a declaration about the first use in commerce, and specimens(s) taht demonstrate the trademark's use in connection with the goods or services.
